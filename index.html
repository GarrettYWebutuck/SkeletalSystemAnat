The Skeletal System

The Skeletal System: An Introduction

The Skeletal System is a network of bones, joints, cartilage, and ligaments(Cords of tissue that hold bones together at joints) throughout a body. They give structure to said body and allow the body to have movement.

The Skeletal System: Major Functions

  • Support

    Bones serve as support for muscles and flesh so that the body has shape. Without the crucial support given by the skeletal system, the body would be just a lump on the floor.

  • Protection

    The Skeletal System helps to protect vital organs, for instance, the ribcage protects the lungs and heart while the brain is protected by the skull. The spinal cord is protected by vertabrae.

  • Movement

    The Skeletal System helps with movement by acting as a solid, inflexible object for muscles to connect to. This allows for more precise movement relative to muscles connected to a more flexible material like cartilage or other muscle.

  • Storage

    The Skeletal System functions as storage for minerals and fat. It stores these materials in a porus section of bone called "marrow". These minerals are essentail for brain function and muscle movement, even blood clotting would be effected if you didnt have the minerals that are stored in marrow.

  • Blood Cell Production

    The Skeletal System functions as a factory of sorts for blood cells. Hematopoiesis (Blood cell formation, hem-ah-to-po-ee-sis) occurs within the marrow of some bones.

The Skeletal System: Major Structures

  • Axial Skeleton

    The Axial Skeleton is the torso and head of the Skeletal System. It houses most if not all of the major organs.

  • Appendicular Skeleton

    The Appendicular Skeleton is the limbs of the Skeletal System. It provides a wide range of movement relative to the Axial Skeleton.
